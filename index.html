How the program works

Lifeline and the Affordable Connectivity Program reduce the cost of phone service for eligible households. Depending on provider and state, you may receive a SIM card, a basic device, and monthly service that includes talk, text, and data. Benefits vary by location and inventory, so always review plan details before you enroll.

Useful references, FCC Lifeline, and USAC Lifeline Support. These pages explain rules, approved documents, and consumer protections.

Who is eligible

  • Household income near or below federal poverty guidelines, check current limits on official pages
  • Or participation in SNAP, Medicaid, SSI, Federal Public Housing Assistance, or similar programs
  • Some tribal households may qualify for enhanced support

Names and addresses must match across all documents, mismatches cause delays.

Documents checklist

  • Government ID, name must match the application
  • Proof of address, recent bill or statement
  • Proof of income or program participation, current dates and clear text

Take clear photos, crop background, and save as JPG or PDF. Keep copies of everything you upload.

Apply in simple steps

1, pick a participating provider in your area, 2, create an account with an email you check daily, 3, complete the form exactly as shown on your ID, 4, upload required documents, both sides if needed, 5, submit and save confirmation emails, 6, after approval, follow activation instructions and test calls, texts, and data.

Provider starting points, AirTalk Wireless, Q Link Wireless, TruConnect, StandUp Wireless. Compare coverage and plan details before applying.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Blurry scans or cropped documents, reviewers must see names, dates, and case numbers
  • Different emails used across steps, use one email from start to finish
  • Expired award letters, always upload the latest version
